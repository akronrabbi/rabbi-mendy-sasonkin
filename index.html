His Life & Legacy

A Life Given to Community

In 1989, Rabbi Menachem Mendel Sasonkin arrived in Akron, Ohio, with a calling and a mission. Together with his wife Kaila, he founded Chabad of Akron & Canton — bringing warmth, learning, and an open door to all who sought it. His home was never truly closed; the Shabbos table always had room for one more.

In 1995, he became the Rabbi of Anshe Sfard Synagogue, a role he would hold with devotion for the next 23 years. Whether leading services, teaching Torah, counseling families, or simply sitting across the table from someone who needed to be heard, Rabbi Sasonkin brought his full self — his humor, his learning, his boundless love for every Jewish soul.

He built a community not through institution, but through relationship. Over three decades, he touched thousands of lives — Jews and non-Jews alike — leaving each person feeling seen, valued, and connected to something larger than themselves.

On Simchat Torah 2018 — his favorite holiday, the day the Torah cycle completes and begins again — Rabbi Mendy Sasonkin passed from this world surrounded by his family. He was 54 years old. His memory is a blessing that will not fade.

He is survived by his beloved wife, Kaila, and their ten children.
